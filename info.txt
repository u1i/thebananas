Here is a technical breakdown clarifying the three models, tracking their underlying architectures and production capabilities:

### Key Architectural Shifts

* **The Baseline (Nano Banana):** Originally designed strictly for speed and low-cost generation. It handles rapid prototyping well but struggles to maintain fidelity when prompted for complex spatial layouts, typography, or multi-character consistency.
* **The Heavyweight (Nano Banana Pro):** Stepping up to the Gemini 3.0 Pro architecture, this model solves the text rendering and spatial logic issues. It easily handles multi-subject references, making it highly consistent, but it comes with higher latency and inference costs.
* **The Pipeline Optimizer (Nano Banana 2):** This model shifts back to a highly efficient Flash architecture (Gemini 3.1 Flash Image) but retains the Pro-tier outputs (like 4K resolution and precise typography). Notably, it introduces real-time web grounding via Google Search to accurately render specific, real-world subjects. It also exposes configurable "thinking levels" (Minimal vs. High/Dynamic), allowing you to manually trade off latency for deeper reasoning during complex prompt execution.
